material preparation

back next

before starting

  • Gather all materials:
  • Calibrated thermometers
  • Yeast packet
  • 5 gallon bucket
  • Sanitizer
  • Brew kettle
  • Single burner/stove
  • Long handled spoon
  • All ingredients
  • Mash tun
  • Irish moss tablets
  • Hydrometer
  • A few cups and flask
  • Copper coils(or your chosen cooling method)

steps

  • Pop yeast packet before starting. Leave at room temperature for 3-12 hours.
  • Make 5 gallon bucket of sanitizer. Drop all tools in.
  • Bring water in brew kettle to 180 degrees.
  • Fill brew kettle with 3 gallons of water + 1.5qt per 2lbs of grain.
back next

mashing

back next

before starting

  • Have thermometer ready.
  • Have 180 degree water ready.
  • Gather the grains.
  • Sanitize mash tun.

steps

  • Add 3 gallons of hot water to mash tun.
  • Stir in the grains slowly.
  • Once all grains are in, take temperature.
  • Add water as needed to hit target temperature range (usually 150-152).
  • START A TIMER FOR 60 MINUTES.
  • Place lid on mash tun and fight the urge to check temperature often.
back next

Sparge

back next

before starting

  • Have brew kettle ready.
  • Have hydrometer ready.
  • Collect large spoon to stir grains.

steps

  • Drain from mash tun into a cup.
  • Pour cup on top of the mash tun until it contains no sediment.
  • Drain mash tun into brew kettle without disturbing the grain bed until empty.
  • Add water and stir gently. Repeat steps above until brew kettle reaches needed volume, typically about 6 gallons for a 5 gallon batch.
  • Take a gravity reading.
back next

boil

back next

before starting

  • Collect hops.
  • Collect irish moss tablets.
  • Gather copper coils, or chosen cooling method.
  • About 6 gallonss of wort should now be in the kettle.
  • During the boil there should be NO lid on the brew kettle.

steps

  • Boil, typically for 60 minutes.
  • Add hops when recipe requires.
  • With 10 minutes remaining in boil time, add coils and irish moss to boiling wort.
back next

cooling

back next

before starting

  • Ensure everything is sanitized.
  • Be careful as you are cooling not to splash or return any samples back to the kettle.

steps

  • Carefully and quickly cool wort to 70 degrees or less.

to the carboy

back next

steps

  • Transfer cooled young beer to carboy and double check the temperature.
  • While draining to carboy, pull a sample for gravity reading and do not pour sample back into carboy.
back next

pitch yeast

back next

before starting

  • Collect yeast.
  • Be extremely careful because any contamination added to the carboy now will remain.

steps

  • Confirm young beer temperature is at target (less than 70 degrees).
  • Pour yeast into carboy.
